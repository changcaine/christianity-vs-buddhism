Key Concept

Christianity

Buddhism

Core Problem

Sin — disobedience to God's will

Ignorance (Avidyā) — misunderstanding the nature of reality

Cause of Suffering

Sin and separation from God

Attachment, craving (Taṇhā), and ignorance

Ultimate Goal

Salvation — eternal life with God in Heaven

Nirvana — liberation from the cycle of rebirth (samsara)

Path to Liberation

Faith in Jesus Christ, grace, repentance, sacraments

Eightfold Path — ethical living, meditation, wisdom

View of God

Monotheistic — personal, omniscient, omnipotent God

Non-theistic — no creator god; emphasis on self-realization

View of the Self

Soul (immortal and individual)

No-self (Anattā) — the self is an illusion

Afterlife

Heaven or Hell based on judgment

Rebirth until enlightenment; then Nirvana

Scriptures

Bible (Old & New Testament)

Tripiṭaka (Pali Canon), Mahayana Sutras

Ethical Foundation

Ten Commandments, Jesus’ teachings (e.g. Sermon on the Mount)

Five Precepts, Noble Eightfold Path

Means of Grace

Divine grace through Christ, sacraments, prayer

Personal effort through meditation, mindfulness, wisdom

Role of Jesus/Buddha

Jesus — Son of God, Savior

Buddha — enlightened teacher, not divine

View of the World

Created by God, inherently good but fallen

Samsara — a cycle of suffering to transcend
